XOR Cipher

The XOR cipher executes an exclusive conjunction (XOR) bitwise for the plaintext and the keyword. Performing another encryption for the ciphertext using the same key results in the plaintext again, due to the symmetric nature of the XOR conjunction. XOR is one of the simplest operations, and is widely used today both in modern block ciphers and stream ciphers.

Classification

The XOR cipher is a polyalphabetic substitution cipher.

Encryption

The XOR algorithm encodes the characters of the plaintext into binary, which are then bitwise XOR-combined, resulting in binary ciphertext characters. The encoding follows the enumeration of the alphabets – the first character is encoded with …0000, the next one with …0001 and so on).

To guarantee, that every result of an XOR combination has a correspondent character in the ciphertext alphabet, this alphabet has to be a length that is a power of two, and must have more or equal elements than the plaintext alphabet.

A … 000
B … 001
C … 010
D … 011
E … 100
F … 101
G … 110
H … 111
